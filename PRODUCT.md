# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- **First-time visitors** — people in or near St. James, Jamaica, curious about the church, looking for service times or directions. Often on mobile, often on a Sunday (they may not know Adventist worship is Saturday). Need immediate answers: when, where, what to expect.
- **Members and regular attenders** — the 463-member congregation checking event schedules, announcements, sermon archives, and prayer requests. Returning visitors who need depth, not orientation.
- **Seekers and digital congregation** — people beyond Jamaica watching livestreams, listening to sermons, or exploring Adventist faith. Need media access and a sense of belonging from a distance.
- **Administrative staff** — church leaders managing content via the admin dashboard. A secondary but regular user group.

## Product Purpose

The Lilliput SDA Church website exists to extend the church's welcome beyond its sanctuary doors. It serves as the digital front door for a 50-year-old congregation in St. James, Jamaica — helping people find service times, watch sermons, connect with ministries, submit prayer requests, and feel the warmth of the community before they ever visit in person.

Success looks like a first-time visitor arriving at the physical church already feeling they belong.

## Brand Personality

Warm, reverent, Jamaican. The tone is dignified but not formal, warm but not saccharine. The site should feel like being greeted at the door by someone who remembers your name — not like reading a brochure.

- **Voice**: Direct, personal, slightly warm. Sentences start with "you" more often than "we."
- **Emotional goal**: A visitor should feel a sense of peace, welcome, and quiet anticipation — the same feeling as walking into a sanctuary where the light is golden and someone is already playing the piano.

## Anti-references

- **Generic mega-church templates**: Polished, professional, and utterly soulless. The kind of site where every photo is a stock image and every headline says "Join Us This Sunday." Lilliput SDA should feel specific, local, and human — not drop-shipped from a template catalog.

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth is the feature** — Every surface should feel like a welcome, not a transaction. The design should make visitors feel personally invited before they read a single word.

2. **Reverent but not distant** — The site serves a sacred purpose but should feel approachable, not austere. Dignity without coldness; warmth without informality.

3. **Community first, information second** — People should see themselves and their neighbors reflected in the design before they read schedules or doctrine.

4. **Jamaican roots, global faith** — The local identity of St. James should be felt in the visuals while remaining accessible to any visitor worldwide.

5. **Every pixel earns its place** — Clutter disrespects the visitor's attention. Strip everything that doesn't serve welcome, worship, or wayfinding.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. The codebase already demonstrates skip-to-content links, focus-visible outlines with sufficient contrast, forced-colors support, reduced-motion compliance, RTL logical properties, and text-sizing that respects browser defaults. Maintain and extend these patterns.
